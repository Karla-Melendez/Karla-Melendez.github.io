Every technical writer should have a portfolio site. I have known 
this for a long time — and yet, like many things we know we should 
do but keep putting off, it took a job search to finally make it 
happen.

This site is the result of several months of work — not just 
building the pages you see here, but rethinking how I present 
myself professionally, what I want to say about my 20+ years of 
experience, and where I want to go next.

## What You Will Find Here

This site has a few distinct purposes:

**Portfolio** — My writing samples are available on the Portfolio 
page, including knowledge base articles, user guides, administrator 
guides, and integration guides drawn from my career as a Senior 
Technical Writer.

**Services** — I am available for freelance technical writing, 
copyediting, proofreading, and English ↔ Spanish translation 
engagements. If you need clear, accurate documentation produced 
by someone who thinks like a developer and writes like a human, 
I would love to hear from you.

**Current Project** — I am building an aquascaping app called 
Aquascape Vision. As an aquarist myself, I have long wanted a 
tool that helps hobbyists design and visualize planted aquariums 
before committing to purchases. The project is in its early stages, 
but the journey of building it is teaching me as much about product 
development as anything else I have done.

**This Blog** — I plan to write here about technical writing, 
documentation best practices, AI tools, and the aquascaping app 
development process. I cannot promise a rigid publishing schedule, 
but I can promise that when I write, it will be worth reading.

## A Note on How This Site Was Built

This site runs on Jekyll and GitHub Pages — tools that are 
increasingly relevant to technical writers working in docs-as-code 
environments. Building it has been its own education in version 
control, Markdown, YAML configuration, and static site publishing. 
I am glad I did it.
